# Healthy Meal Ideas and Recipes

## Quick Breakfast Ideas (5-10 minutes)

### Protein-Packed Smoothie
**Ingredients:**
- 1 cup unsweetened almond milk
- 1 scoop protein powder (vanilla or berry)
- 1/2 banana
- 1 cup spinach (you won't taste it!)
- 1 tbsp almond butter
- Ice cubes

**Instructions:**
Blend all ingredients until smooth. Add water if too thick.

**Nutrition:** ~300 calories, 25g protein, 5g fiber

### Overnight Oats
**Ingredients:**
- 1/2 cup rolled oats
- 1/2 cup Greek yogurt
- 1/2 cup milk of choice
- 1 tbsp chia seeds
- 1 tbsp honey
- Berries and nuts for topping

**Instructions:**
Mix all ingredients except toppings. Refrigerate overnight. Add toppings in morning.

## Lunch Ideas (15-20 minutes prep)

### Mediterranean Bowl
**Base:** Quinoa or brown rice
**Protein:** Grilled chicken, chickpeas, or feta cheese
**Vegetables:** Cucumber, tomatoes, red onion, olives
**Dressing:** Olive oil, lemon juice, oregano
**Extras:** Hummus, avocado

### Mason Jar Salads (Meal Prep)
**Layer from bottom up:**
1. Dressing (2 tbsp)
2. Hard vegetables (carrots, peppers)
3. Protein (chicken, beans, cheese)
4. Grains (quinoa, farro)
5. Soft vegetables (tomatoes, cucumber)
6. Greens (lettuce, spinach)

**Keeps fresh for 3-4 days**

## Dinner Ideas (30-45 minutes)

### Sheet Pan Salmon and Vegetables
**Ingredients:**
- 4 salmon fillets
- 2 cups broccoli florets
- 1 bell pepper, sliced
- 1 zucchini, sliced
- Olive oil, salt, pepper, garlic powder

**Instructions:**
1. Preheat oven to 425°F
2. Toss vegetables with oil and seasonings
3. Place on sheet pan with salmon
4. Bake 12-15 minutes

### Turkey and Vegetable Stir-Fry
**Ingredients:**
- 1 lb ground turkey (93% lean)
- 2 cups mixed frozen vegetables
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- Brown rice for serving

**Instructions:**
1. Cook turkey in large pan until browned
2. Add garlic and vegetables
3. Stir in soy sauce and sesame oil
4. Cook until vegetables are tender
5. Serve over brown rice

## Meal Prep Tips

### Sunday Prep Session (2-3 hours)
1. **Proteins:** Grill chicken, bake fish, hard-boil eggs
2. **Grains:** Cook quinoa, brown rice, or farro in batches
3. **Vegetables:** Wash and chop raw veggies, roast others
4. **Snacks:** Portion nuts, wash fruits, make energy balls

### Storage Tips
- Use glass containers for better freshness
- Keep dressings separate until ready to eat
- Freeze portions for longer storage
- Label containers with contents and date

### Make-Ahead Freezer Meals
- Breakfast burritos (wrap individually)
- Soup portions (freeze in mason jars)
- Smoothie packs (pre-portioned frozen fruits)
- Meatballs or burger patties

## Budget-Friendly Options

### Protein Sources
- Eggs ($2-3/dozen)
- Dried beans and lentils ($1-2/lb)
- Canned tuna or salmon ($1-2/can)
- Chicken thighs (often cheaper than breasts)
- Greek yogurt (buy large containers)

### Seasonal Shopping
- Buy fruits and vegetables in season
- Frozen vegetables are nutritious and affordable
- Buy grains and nuts in bulk
- Use store brands for basics

### Batch Cooking Saves Money
- Cook large portions and freeze extras
- Use versatile ingredients in multiple meals
- Make your own salad dressings and sauces
- Repurpose leftovers into new meals

## Special Dietary Considerations

### Vegetarian/Vegan Options
- Emphasize plant proteins (beans, lentils, quinoa)
- Include B12 supplement if vegan
- Focus on iron-rich foods with vitamin C
- Use nutritional yeast for B vitamins and flavor

### Gluten-Free Options
- Focus on naturally gluten-free grains (rice, quinoa)
- Read labels carefully
- Use alternative flours for baking
- Many whole foods are naturally gluten-free

### Low-Carb Options
- Emphasize proteins and healthy fats
- Use vegetable substitutes (cauliflower rice, zucchini noodles)
- Include plenty of non-starchy vegetables
- Monitor portion sizes of nuts and seeds
