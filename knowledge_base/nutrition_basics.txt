# Nutrition Fundamentals

## Macronutrients Basics

### Proteins
**Daily Goal:** 0.8-1.2g per kg of body weight (more for active individuals)
**Sources:**
- Lean meats (chicken, turkey, lean beef)
- Fish and seafood
- Eggs
- Dairy products (Greek yogurt, cottage cheese)
- Plant proteins (beans, lentils, quinoa, tofu)
- Nuts and seeds

**Benefits:**
- Muscle building and repair
- Satiety and appetite control
- Metabolic boost

### Carbohydrates  
**Daily Goal:** 45-65% of total calories
**Best Sources:**
- Whole grains (oats, brown rice, quinoa)
- Fruits and vegetables
- Legumes
- Sweet potatoes

**Timing:**
- Pre-workout: 1-2 hours before exercise
- Post-workout: Within 30 minutes for recovery

### Healthy Fats
**Daily Goal:** 20-35% of total calories
**Sources:**
- Avocados
- Nuts and seeds
- Olive oil
- Fatty fish (salmon, mackerel)
- Nut butters

## Meal Planning Basics

### Balanced Plate Method
- 1/2 plate: Non-starchy vegetables
- 1/4 plate: Lean protein
- 1/4 plate: Complex carbohydrates
- Add healthy fats (1-2 tablespoons)

### Sample Daily Meal Plan (2000 calories)

**Breakfast:**
- 1 cup oatmeal with berries and nuts
- 1 Greek yogurt
- Green tea or coffee

**Lunch:**
- Grilled chicken salad with mixed vegetables
- Quinoa or brown rice
- Olive oil vinaigrette

**Snack:**
- Apple with almond butter
- Or Greek yogurt with berries

**Dinner:**
- Baked salmon
- Roasted vegetables
- Sweet potato

**Hydration:**
- 8-10 glasses of water daily
- More during exercise
- Herbal teas count toward fluid intake

## Weight Management

### For Weight Loss:
- Create moderate calorie deficit (300-500 calories)
- Focus on protein to maintain muscle
- Eat plenty of vegetables for volume and nutrients
- Control portions mindfully

### For Muscle Gain:
- Slight calorie surplus (200-500 calories)
- Higher protein intake (1.6-2.2g per kg body weight)
- Time protein around workouts
- Include complex carbs for energy

### For Maintenance:
- Eat at maintenance calories
- Focus on nutrient density
- Include variety in food choices
- Practice mindful eating

## Pre and Post Workout Nutrition

### Pre-Workout (1-2 hours before):
- Banana with peanut butter
- Oatmeal with fruit
- Greek yogurt with berries
- Toast with honey

### Post-Workout (within 30 minutes):
- Protein shake with fruit
- Chocolate milk
- Greek yogurt with granola
- Turkey and cheese wrap

## Supplements (Consult healthcare provider first)
- Multivitamin for insurance
- Vitamin D if deficient
- Omega-3 if not eating fish regularly
- Protein powder for convenience (not necessary)
- Creatine for strength training (3-5g daily)

## Healthy Cooking Tips
- Meal prep on weekends
- Use herbs and spices for flavor
- Steam, bake, or grill instead of frying
- Keep healthy snacks readily available
- Read nutrition labels
- Cook at home when possible
